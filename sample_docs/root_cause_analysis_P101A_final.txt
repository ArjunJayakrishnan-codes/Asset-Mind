ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS — FINAL REPORT
Equipment: Pump P-101A
Prepared By: QA Engineer, reviewed by Plant Manager
Related Work Orders: WO-11042, WO-11208, WO-11390, WO-11675
Related Permits: PTW-55210, PTW-55388
Reference Standards: ISO 10816, ISO 21049, OISD-STD-105

Sequence of events reconstructed from maintenance logs, inspection reports,
and incident records:
1) Jan 10, 2024 — routine maintenance logs early upward vibration trend
   (1.4 to 2.1 mm/s RMS) on P-101A outboard bearing.
2) Mar 22, 2024 — mechanical seal weep first observed and logged as a minor
   deviation; seal replacement deferred to next planned shutdown rather
   than treated as an early failure indicator, contrary to OEM guidance.
3) Q1/Q2 2024 inspection reports both flag adverse trending; Q2 raises a
   formal non-conformance escalated to Plant Manager.
4) Jun 18, 2024 — near-miss high-vibration trip, still not resulting in
   immediate seal replacement.
5) Aug 2, 2024 — mechanical seal failure and breakdown; near-miss for fire
   given proximity to hot-work zone; standby pump P-101B covers process
   load with no interruption.
Root cause: deferred maintenance response to a documented, multi-month
vibration and seal-weep trend, compounded by an escalation process that did
not treat repeated minor deviations as a cumulative risk signal. Corrective
action: automated vibration-threshold escalation (see 2024 Compliance Audit
Report) and revision of SOP P-101A Startup and Shutdown to mandate
immediate seal replacement on any weep detection rather than deferral.
