INCIDENT REPORT - Coke Oven Battery Area
Document Type: Incident Report
Date: 12-Jan-2025
Prepared by: Safety Officer

Equipment involved: HX-2201, V-405, P-101A
Summary: A gas leak was detected near the coke oven battery. Entrapped gases accumulated
following a delay in scheduled maintenance on HX-2201. Gas pressure sensors flagged an
anomalous reading approximately 40 minutes before the explosion, but no automated
system connected the sensor reading to an evacuation trigger.

Related Permit: PTW-88213 (hot work) was active within 30 meters of V-405 at the time
of the incident, overlapping with the gas accumulation zone. This is a compound risk
condition per OISD-STD-105.

Root cause: near-miss reports for HX-2201 from March 2024 (WO-10234) described a similar
gas pressure deviation that was resolved without root-cause investigation. The failure
pattern recurred.

Corrective actions: mandatory cross-check between active hot work permits and live gas
sensor thresholds within 50m radius. Reference: Factory Act 1948, DGMS Circular on
confined space entry.
